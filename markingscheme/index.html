Marking Scheme

Marking Scheme

Program Element Marks
Introductory Exercise 3%
Error Analysis Quiz 6%
Python Problem Set (2) 10%
Python Exercises (5) 25%
Instruments and Wiring (2) 10%
Experiments (9 weights) 45%
Experiment Survey 1%

Introductory Exercise

You are required to complete one exercise early in the term. The “Oscillations of a Hoop” exercise.

Python Problem Set

You will write and submit the answers to all questions. Your code should work and it should print out all relevant values. You should also provide those numbers in you submission.

Error Analysis Quiz

You will take a 45 minute Error Analysis Quiz. The quiz will be based on the Error Analysis package you learned in your first year. To prepare, follow this link: www.faraday.utoronto.ca/PVB/Harrison/ErrorAnalysis/index.html

Computational Exercises

There will be 5 exercises, based on Python programming. You will have 1 session each for 4 exercises, and 2 sessions for the final exercise. You are expected to treat these exercises in the same way as experiments later in the course – Taking notes in your lab book of the data collection and observation, writing and commenting Python code to perform the data analysis and fitting, and writing a summary or conclusion discussing your results.

Instruments and Wiring

You will do two lab exercises aimed at refreshing your experimental skills (using instruments, wiring a simple DC circuit).

Experiments

You will choose experiments totaling at least 9 weights from a selection of 15-20 experiments. The weights ratings given to experiments should help to guide you as to the depth of experimentation typically carried out by students in this laboratory. In selecting your experiments and weights as well as when planning your experiment, you should manage your time carefully. Evaluation of experiments will be done by the demonstrator responsible for the individual experiment. For most of your experiments, you will be evaluated using a mix of in-lab assessment and lab notebook evaluation. If time permits you can complete additional experiments and replace lower experiments marks with higher ones.

Experiment Survey

An experiment survey will be released to allow you to select your preferences for the independent experiments in the last half of the course. Completing this survey is useful for planning the availability of apparatus and space in the lab, and for understanding which labs are popular. There is no correct answer, so the grade is awarded for submitting the survey before the deadline.
